Marxism or socialism may converge to dictatorship when centralized party authority, ideological certainty, and weak institutional checks combine. In such systems, dissent can be reclassified as betrayal of the collective project. Once opposition is delegitimized, executive and party power expand, and coercion becomes easier to justify as necessary for equality or transition. However, this is not a logical necessity of every socialist idea; it is a recurrent political risk under conditions of concentrated authority, weak pluralism, and limited accountability.
