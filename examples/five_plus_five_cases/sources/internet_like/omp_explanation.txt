Orthogonal Matching Pursuit (OMP) is a greedy sparse approximation algorithm.
At each iteration, it selects the atom with the highest correlation to the current residual.
The support set is updated, then a least-squares problem is solved over the selected atoms.
The residual is recomputed, and the process repeats until a stopping criterion is met.
OMP is widely used in compressed sensing because it is simple and often effective.
